1 – Systems

Heritage Conservation

A system mapping of how to preserve a historical site

What is the system?

The system of heritage conservation aims to preserve historical sites for future generations. It involves historical sites themselves, visitor interactions, research activities, and policy decisions. Feedback loops, such as reinforcing or balancing loops, shape the system. Reinforcing loops occur when successful conservation interventions attract more resources and visitors. Balancing loops arise when negative impacts prompt restrictions for long-term sustainability. The frequency and delay of feedback loops are crucial. Rapid feedback allows for prompt adjustments, while longer delay loops require proactive measures. Identifying leverage points, such as policy changes prioritizing sustainability or community involvement, is essential.

Architects and conservation professionals play a crucial role. They assess conditions, develop plans, and oversee projects, working with craftsmen and construction teams. Traditional techniques and materials are used for authenticity, with careful craftsmanship and attention to detail. The system of heritage conservation requires a delicate balance between preservation and accessibility. Overemphasis on visitors may compromise authenticity, while excessive restrictions limit engagement. In conclusion, heritage conservation is a complex system involving historical sites, feedback loops, architects, professionals, and construction processes. Balancing preservation and accessibility, utilizing feedback loops effectively, and engaging skilled professionals are key to preserving the significance and integrity of heritage sites.

Group Work

By Mengyu Wang, Hongqian Li, Yilin Wang

In recent years, advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) have revolutionized various industries, including design and architecture. One notable area of progress lies in utilizing AI models to foster collaboration between professionals and the general public. By acting as a bridge, AI can facilitate the translation of creative ideas from non-professionals into a format that professionals can understand and implement. This essay explores the significance of shared authorship in design and how AI models such as pix2pix GAN and Deep Convolutional Generative Adversarial Networks (DCGAN) contribute to this process. Through the examples of an interactive community park design platform and the DeepScope project, we will discuss how AI enables citizens to actively participate in the design process and enhance the quality of urban spaces.

The Power of Shared Authorship: Shared authorship, which involves the collaboration between professionals and the public, offers numerous benefits in the design process. It empowers communities by allowing them to voice their ideas and actively participate in shaping their environments. By involving citizens, professionals gain valuable insights and ensure that designs align with the needs and preferences of the intended users.

The Role of AI as a Bridge: AI models such as pix2pix GAN and DCGAN act as powerful tools to bridge the gap between professionals and non-professionals in the design process. These models enable the translation of non-technical ideas into professional renderings, making it easier for professionals to comprehend and implement the concepts proposed by the public. a. Pix2pix GAN: The interactive community park design platform described in the research paper demonstrates the capabilities of the pix2pix GAN model. Through the platform, citizens can sketch their visions for the park using different colors, expressing their creativity without the need for technical skills. The model then translates these sketches into technical plan renderings, allowing non-professionals to visualize their ideas in a professional format. This translation process ensures that the collaborative design process becomes more inclusive and accessible.

b. DCGAN in the DeepScope Project: The Media Lab's DeepScope project takes a different approach, utilizing DCGAN and a Tangible User Interface (TUI) to facilitate real-time city prototype designing and visualization. By using Lego models, users can construct physical representations of urban design concepts. These Lego models are then processed by the DCGAN, which generates perspective renderings of the proposed designs. This tangible and interactive approach encourages citizens to actively engage in the design process and provides them with a clearer understanding of how their ideas can be realized.

Fostering Creativity and Implementing Ideas:The implementation of AI models in collaborative design not only enhances the participation of the public but also fosters creativity. By leveraging AI to transform non-professional ideas into professional renderings, citizens can see their visions come to life in a tangible and visually appealing format. This process encourages further ideation and exploration, empowering individuals to contribute actively to the design of their community spaces.

In conclusion, the utilization of AI models such as pix2pix GAN and DCGAN in collaborative design processes allows for shared authorship between professionals and the public. AI acts as a bridge, transforming non-professional ideas into a format that professionals can comprehend and implement. Through the examples of the interactive community park design platform and the DeepScope project, we have seen how AI empowers citizens to actively participate in the design process, fosters creativity, and enhances the quality of urban spaces. As we move forward, embracing AI in design can contribute to more inclusive and innovative solutions, ensuring that the voices of all stakeholders are heard and respected.

3 – Politics

NYC Museum Mapping

A spatial analysis with multiple forms of data.

"DATA MANIFESTO"

To gain a deeper understanding of the cultural context and exhibition status of New York's museums and galleries, I will collect data based on various aspects such as their geographic location, floor space, historical age, and the number and provenance of their collections.

These data can be obtained by visiting the Museum Alliance organization, the museum's official website and asking ChatGPT. Of these, the data from the official website is more accurate and instantaneous, but it is not fully accessible to the public, and the amount of data is too large to be quickly processed. The data given by ChatGPT is more abbreviated. Still, it needs to be more accurate and real-time, and its visualization can only be used for rough narratives, not for quantitative research and analysis.

A 2017 survey report by the American Alliance of Museums shows that museums still have many inequalities regarding gender, race, and collecting tendencies. Hence, the museum ecology embodied in these data continues to result from directional selection by the art-collecting community under the dominant system of Western male discourse.

Thus, these data are undoubtedly biased. When dealing with data in the historical and cultural realm constructed from narratives of inequality, we should always be wary of racial and gender discrimination and colonialism lurking in the data.

"A 2017 survey by the American Alliance of Museums"
"Orgnizational Characteristics"

Method-1: Javascript/P5

"TOP20 NYC MUSEUM"

By matching the data coordinate system with the background map coordinate system, the locations of the museums are visualized on the NYC map. The marker changes color by defining the mouse proximity action and shows the museum's name, the first column in the data file. I also connected the two museums and added dynamic airplanes to deliver the transportation distance between them.

Method-2: Grasshopper

"Average Exhibition Space"

By importing the area data of the top 20 museums in New York and the number of artworks data provided by GPT into Grasshopper, we abstract the average exhibition area occupied by each artwork into a rectangular space. As the camera passes through the juxtaposed exhibition spaces of different area sizes, how the spatial scales influence the experience of seeing an exhibition is visualized on the screen.

"METHOD MANIFESTO"

The selected tools and methods harmoniously align with the visualization goals. JavaScript and P5 provide interactivity, enhancing the user experience and allowing users to interact with the map and explore the museums and artworks. The use of CSV data ensures seamless data management. At the same time, Grasshopper's analytical capabilities allow for insightful data analysis, leading to a deeper understanding of the exhibition spaces in the NYC museums. Lastly, incorporating GIFs enhances data communication, making the findings more accessible and visually appealing to a broader audience.

In summary, this visualization practice successfully leverages a well-chosen set of tools and methods to create an interactive map and visualize exhibition space data for NYC museums. Through this approach, the practice makes complex data more accessible, engaging, and informative, offering valuable insights into the distribution of exhibition spaces in the diverse and culturally rich art scene of New York City.
